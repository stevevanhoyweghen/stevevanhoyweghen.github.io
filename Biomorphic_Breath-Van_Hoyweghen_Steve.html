Artwork Description

Audience Experience

The audience sees a 3 by 4 meter silver reflecting foil laying on the ground. The foil gently moves up and down in a slow, modulated organic motion. The movement sequence pattern is changing over time. The spectators will only hear some low noise fan buzzing and a crackling sound of the silver foil and the plastic bags movement.

Naming Clarification

The artwork gives the impression of a breathing creature. I choose the name “Biomorphic Breath #1” referring to:

  • •.Biomorphism1. 

  • •.The motion dynamics can be associated with the act of breathing. 

  • •.The sequence number #1 identifies this work uniquely if similar works are created in the future. 

Construction and Behavior

  • •.Eight 60 liter trash bags are laying on the ground in a grid pattern. 

  • •.Each trash bag has two fans mounted back to back. The fans are placed in a tube made from PET bottles. This makes it possible to inflate and deflate the bags under software control. The fans are from abandoned CPU-coolers. Rubber bandages keep the bags in place. 

  • •.The CPU-cooler fans are controlled with Pulse Width Modulation2. The PWM-signals are controlled with a micro-controller programmed in C++. The software is programmed by me. 

  • •.The micro-controller is connected with 16 fans in total to control the inflate-deflate cycle of each bag individually. Different predetermined sequences or randomly generated sequences are possible. The duration of the inflate-deflate cycle can be controlled within certain limits. 

  • •.On top of the bags are four crackled connected Mylar emergency foil blankets, each measuring 160 cm by 210 cm. The foils are pushed up and down by the inflate-deflate cycles of the trash bags. The maximum height movement variation is about 50 cm. 

  • •.The whole construction is fixed with a construction of recycled tent poles from old tents no longer in use. 

  • •.The is an extension build with a display and two ultrasonic sensors allowing the audience to interact with the installation. This supports: 

      • ▪.Selecting different programmed inflate-deflate sequences. 

      • ▪.Select a random sequence. 

      • ▪.Change the intensity of the inflation-deflation sequences by moving a hand closer-farther from an ultrasonic transducer. 

      • ▪.Change the pace of the inflation-deflation sequences by moving a hand closer-farther from an ultrasonic transducer.

Followed Mindset en Philosophy

  • •.Reuse and up-cycle 

    • The kinetic artwork is mostly made from e-waste and cheap household items that are transformed in an engaging audience experience. I contacted the local waste handling company and a computer shop asking for old computer power supplies and fans.

  • •.Flexible and modular 

    • The bags with back to back fans are a cheap way to do lightweight control of fabrics, foils, paper, etc. that can be combined in several ways.

  • •.Low-cost 

    • Using abandoned fans sourced from unused processor coolers, an old desktop power supply, cheap household items, throwaway items, standard UTP cabling, a cheap micro-controller, is resulting in a limited overall cost.

  • •.Lightweight 

    • The whole installation can be transported very compact and is easy to carry around, minimizing transport and setup costs.

  • •.Open source 

    • Once implemented and presented to the public I will document and share this project under Creative Commons5 to enable other artists to use this concept for their own goals.

  • •.Easy to install and repair if needed 

    • An installation and trouble shooting guide will be provided if something goes wrong. Because the materials used are low-cost, backup materials will be provided.

Installation Requirements

  • •.A 220 Volts power socket on the floor level that is ‘stumble safe’. Power requirements are low, so one standard power socket with an earth pin is sufficient. 

  • •.Nice to have, but not essential. The silver side of Mylar sheet is highly reflective. This opens the potential to put spotlights on the Mylar sheets that can be reflected to the ceiling. This however depends on the setting, ceiling color, ceiling height, available lighting, etc. Please contact me beforehand to know if this possibility can be exploited.
